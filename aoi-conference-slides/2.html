2-Morse theory

Consider a real smooth compact manifold MMM, with a metric ggg and a pair of functions f1,f2f_1, f_2f1​,f2​ such that the corresponding gradient vector fields vi=grad(fi)v_i = \text{grad}(f_i)vi​=grad(fi​) commute with each other.

Then, we would like to consider the space of ''leaves'' - i.e. mappings X:R2→MX: \mathbb{R}^2 \rightarrow MX:R2→M satisfying the property dX(∂/∂xi)=vi for i=1,2dX(\partial/\partial x_i) = v_i \text{ for } i = 1,2dX(∂/∂xi​)=vi​ for i=1,2

Motivation: Andrei Losev's program of 2-dimensional enumerative geometry.

Main topic of the talk: combinatorial nature of this problem is completely analogous to AoI despite them being two completely different geometric settings.

Status of the results: typically, they involve only formal counting of the dimensions. There is no "generic position" property to make these results truly geometric yet.
