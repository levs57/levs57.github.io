Closing remarks

One of the big obstacles in even approaching the original goal of Losev's program is the fact that the notion of complex surface is unstable, in particular, there are no surfaces in a generic almost-complex manifold.

Similar situation is observed with a pair of vector fields: in particular, if we deform them in a generic fashion, the notion of a leaf ceases to exists.

However, interestingly, the formal combinatorial nature of this problem appears to not be wild, on the contrary, it completely mimics GMW, which is a well posed problem on an elliptic equation.

I would like to suggest that actually we might be able to "choose a gauge" - instead of considering the (functional) equation dX−v1dx1−v2dx2=0dX - v_1 dx_1 - v_2 dx_2 = 0dX−v1​dx1​−v2​dx2​=0, we should consider the equation of second order measuring leaves that minimize "non-integrability" (I'm not sure what is the exact form of such action, but it is clear that it must exist).

Then, in case of commuting vector fields, the space of solutions will be the same, but the problem will become well-posed.

Moreover, Mikhail Kapranov has shown me the argument that reconstructs the flat structure on the solution of GMW problem XXX from its image. If similar argument is available for such an elliptic equation I propose, then the combinatorics of the AoI will also likely survive.
