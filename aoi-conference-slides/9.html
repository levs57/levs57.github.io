2-Morse theory

Natural question: in GMW, it is shown that g\mathfrak{g}g is a model of Hochschild cohomology of the algebra of concave paths in AAA (it is denoted g∞\mathfrak{g^{\infty}}g∞ in the work of Kontsevich, Kapranov and Soibelman but I will denote it g+\mathfrak{g}^+g+).

Moreover, deformation of g+\mathfrak{g}^+g+ using MC element ϕ\phiϕ corresponds to the relative Fukaya category.

Question: what is the geometric sense of this deformation in 222-Morse?

Conjecture: it is a category with exceptional collection corresponding to the critical points (of field v1v_1v1​), and morphisms Hom(pi,pj)\text{Hom}(p_i, p_j)Hom(pi​,pj​) for i<ji<ji<j realized as singular chains of the space of (possibly degenerate) trajectories of v1v_1v1​ between iii and jjj.

I will refer to this as "Morse category" in what follows.

So, ideologically I expect that 222-Morse theory in some sense uses second field (v2v_2v2​) to find a combinatorial / cellular structure on the spaces of trajectories of the first field.
